# Foot Solutions — Services

## The Foot Solutions Experience

Every visit starts with a conversation. Our staff — board-certified pedorthists and trained foot wellness specialists — take time to understand your pain, lifestyle, activity level, and health history before recommending anything. No high-pressure sales. Just expert guidance.

A typical visit includes:

1. **Consultation** — We listen. Where is the pain? When does it hurt? What activities do you do? How long have you had this condition?
2. **Foot & Gait Analysis** — Using state-of-the-art digital scanning and pressure mapping technology, we map your feet and identify areas of imbalance, pressure concentration, and misalignment.
3. **Product Recommendation** — Based on the analysis and consultation, we recommend the right custom orthotic and/or supportive footwear from our curated brand selection.
4. **Fitting** — We fit the product to ensure it works properly in the shoe, with your gait, and for your condition.
5. **Ongoing Support** — Foot Solutions stores offer in-house workshops with grinding, heat molding, and bending tools to adjust orthotics as needed. Follow-up visits are part of the relationship.

**Walk-ins welcome at all Georgia locations.** For complex conditions or custom orthotics, calling ahead for a dedicated consultation slot is recommended.

## Custom Foot & Gait Analysis

The cornerstone of the Foot Solutions experience. We use:

- **3D laser foot scanning**: Creates a precise digital map of your arch height, foot length/width, and pressure distribution
- **Gait analysis**: Observes and records how you walk — stride length, foot strike pattern, ankle roll (pronation/supination), knee and hip alignment
- **Pressure mapping**: Identifies hot spots — areas of excessive pressure that cause pain or long-term damage

This is the same technology used by sports medicine professionals and orthopedic clinics. At Foot Solutions, it guides our recommendation — it's not a sales prop.

**The analysis is free** and takes 15–30 minutes. Results are used to design your custom orthotic and to select the right shoe width, cushioning level, and support structure.

## Custom Orthotics — The Flagship Service

Custom orthotics are replacement insoles designed exclusively for your feet by a professional after a thorough evaluation. Unlike off-the-shelf insoles (which are mass-produced for an "average" foot), a true custom orthotic:

- Is individually designed after digital scanning and gait evaluation
- Uses materials selected for your specific condition and lifestyle
- Incorporates the right cushioning density, arch support height, heel cup depth, and forefoot padding
- Is manufactured in-house using 3D printing (not sent to an outside lab)
- Fits your specific shoe type (dress, athletic, work boot, casual)

**Price:** $400–$500 for custom orthotics (varies by complexity)
**Lifespan:** 3+ years with proper care, depending on activity level
**Coverage:** Many HSA/FSA accounts cover custom orthotics — check with your plan

**Custom vs. "customized":** Many retailers sell "customized" insoles, where a salesperson selects from a small range of prefab options after a brief questionnaire. This is NOT the same as a true custom orthotic. Foot Solutions builds each orthotic from scratch based on your individual scan — there is no off-the-shelf starting point.

**Who benefits most from custom orthotics?**
- Plantar fasciitis (especially persistent cases that haven't responded to OTC insoles)
- Flat feet / severe overpronation
- Bunions (to redistribute pressure away from the affected joint)
- Post-tibial tendonitis
- Diabetes-related foot complications
- Runners, hikers, and athletes with biomechanical imbalances
- Anyone who has tried OTC insoles without sufficient relief

## Footwear Fitting & Selection

Not all supportive shoes are created equal — and the wrong shoe can make a foot condition worse, even if it feels comfortable at first. Our staff are trained to identify:

- Correct shoe width (many Americans wear the wrong width)
- Appropriate heel drop for your gait type
- Toe box depth and width for conditions like bunions or hammer toes
- Sole stiffness appropriate for your arch profile
- Cushioning type (EVA foam vs. gel vs. memory foam)

We carry 10+ orthopedic footwear brands in-store and stock an even broader selection online (shop.footsolutions.com).

## What to Expect After Getting Orthotics

**Break-in period:** Most patients need 1–2 weeks to fully adapt to custom orthotics. Start by wearing them 2–3 hours per day and gradually increase. Mild arch aching is normal during the first week.

**Adjustment visits:** Foot Solutions stores have in-house modification tools. If your orthotic creates a pressure point or doesn't feel right after the break-in period, bring it back — we adjust at no additional charge.

**Shoe rotation:** Orthotics can be transferred between shoes of the same type. We recommend having 2–3 pairs of compatible shoes to rotate through.

**Long-term care:** Wipe orthotics clean with a damp cloth. Do not machine wash. Store in a dry environment. Replace every 3–5 years or when the support material shows visible compression.
