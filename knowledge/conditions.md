# Foot Conditions — Scientific Guide & Foot Solutions Approach

## The Foot's Complexity

The human foot contains 26 bones, 33 joints, and 7,000 nerve endings. It is the body's foundation — when alignment is off at the foot, the effects ripple upward through the ankle, knee, hip, and spine. Many people with chronic back pain, knee osteoarthritis, or hip misalignment trace the root cause to a foot biomechanics issue.

---

## Plantar Fasciitis

**What it is:** The plantar fascia is a thick band of fibrous tissue that runs along the bottom of the foot, connecting the heel bone (calcaneus) to the ball of the foot. When overstretched — during running, standing, or walking — micro-tears accumulate in the fascia, causing inflammation and severe heel or arch pain.

**Symptoms:**
- Sharp, stabbing pain in the heel or arch, worst with the first steps in the morning
- Pain that eases after a few minutes of walking but returns after long periods of standing
- Aching that worsens after (not during) exercise
- Tenderness when pressing on the bottom of the heel

**Why it hurts in the morning:** The plantar fascia tightens and shortens during sleep. The first steps re-stretch it suddenly, causing pain. As it warms up, the pain decreases — but the underlying inflammation remains.

**Root causes:** Overpronation (flat feet), high arches, sudden increase in activity, worn-out footwear, tight Achilles tendon, obesity, prolonged standing on hard surfaces.

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- OTC insoles: Plantar Fasciitis Pro FS106 ($119.99) — maximum heel support and arch tension relief
- Custom orthotics: Designed with a tight arch contour to reduce tension on the inflamed fascia, plus heel cushioning to absorb impact
- Footwear: Shoes with firm heel counters and adequate arch support (Brooks, Aetrex, Vionic recommended)
- Night splints may be recommended alongside orthotics for severe cases

**Timeline for relief:** With proper orthotics and footwear, most plantar fasciitis patients see significant improvement in 4–8 weeks.

## Overpronation and Flat Feet

**What it is:** Pronation is the natural inward roll of the foot during walking. Overpronation occurs when the foot rolls too far inward — the arch collapses excessively, placing excessive stress on tendons, ligaments, and joints from the foot through the knee and hip.

**Symptoms:**
- Arch flattening when standing
- Inner ankle pain
- Knee pain (especially inner knee)
- Hip and lower back pain
- Shoes wear down unevenly on the inner edge

**Types:**
- Flexible flat feet: Arch is visible when sitting but collapses when standing — the most common type, often runs in families
- Rigid flat feet: Arch is flat even when non-weight-bearing — less common, sometimes linked to tarsal coalition or arthritis

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- Custom orthotics: Designed with a medial arch post and rearfoot control element to prevent excessive pronation without overcorrecting
- Footwear: Motion control or stability shoes (Brooks Beast GTS, Brooks Ariel GTS, Xelero) with firm medial post in the midsole
- OTC insoles: Active FS104 or ComfortPro FS102 provide transitional support while awaiting custom orthotics

## Supination (Underpronation / High Arches)

**What it is:** The opposite of overpronation — the foot rolls outward during the gait cycle. People with high arches often supinate. The arch acts like a rigid lever rather than a shock absorber, concentrating impact on the outer heel and ball of the foot.

**Symptoms:**
- Lateral ankle instability (prone to ankle sprains)
- Ball-of-foot pain
- Shin splints
- Calluses on outer edge of foot
- Knee and IT band pain

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- Custom orthotics with cushioning beneath the lateral arch and metatarsal heads
- Neutral or cushioned shoes with no motion control features (which would worsen supination)
- Extra cushioning: NOENE insoles, or Kenkoh massage sandals for daily wear relief

## Bunions (Hallux Valgus)

**What it is:** A bunion is a bony protrusion at the base of the big toe joint (the first metatarsophalangeal joint). The big toe drifts toward the second toe, causing the metatarsal head to jut outward. This creates friction, pressure, and pain inside shoes — and can lead to arthritis in the joint over time.

**Tailor's bunion (bunionette):** A similar deformity on the outer edge of the foot at the base of the little toe.

**Causes:** Predominantly genetic (family history is the strongest predictor). Poorly fitted footwear — especially narrow, pointed toe boxes — accelerates progression significantly. Affects women more than men due to shoe styles.

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- Wide toe box footwear is critical — allows the toes to spread without pressing against the deformity (Finn Comfort, Mephisto, Naot, and Drew Shoes offer wide options)
- Custom orthotics redistribute pressure away from the bunion joint, slow progression, and restore motion of the MTP joint
- Bunion pads/cushions for immediate friction relief
- Custom orthotics can significantly slow progression but cannot reverse a bunion — surgery is the only way to permanently correct the bony deformity

**Product recommendation:** Finn Comfort Gomera ($294.99) — designed with anatomically wide toe box for bunion relief. Aetrex Jillian for women also available.

## Hammer Toes, Mallet Toes, and Claw Toes

**What they are:** Deformities of the lesser toes (2nd through 5th) where joints bend abnormally, causing the toes to look curled or claw-like.
- **Hammer toe:** Bend at the middle joint — the toe looks like an upside-down V
- **Mallet toe:** Bend at the end joint — tip of the toe points downward
- **Claw toe:** Both joints bend — the entire toe curls under

**Causes:** Tight shoes, muscle imbalance, flat feet or high arches, nerve damage (diabetes, Charcot-Marie-Tooth), arthritis.

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- Shoes with deep, wide toe boxes (essential — tight shoes are the primary aggravator)
- Custom orthotics to address the underlying arch imbalance contributing to toe deformity
- Toe spacers or hammer toe crest pads to offload pressure from bent joint
- Early intervention with proper footwear can slow or stop progression in flexible hammer toes

## Metatarsalgia (Ball-of-Foot Pain)

**What it is:** Inflammation and pain in the metatarsal heads — the ball of the foot, just behind the toes. Often called "stone bruise" by patients.

**Causes:** High heels, thin-soled shoes, high arches (which shift weight to the metatarsal heads), excess body weight, high-impact activities, Morton's toe.

**Symptoms:** Sharp, aching, or burning pain in the ball of the foot. Numbness or tingling in the toes. Pain that worsens when standing, walking, or flexing the foot.

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- Metatarsal pad in orthotic or insole to offload pressure from the metatarsal heads and redistribute it proximally
- Cushioned footwear with wide toe box
- Kenkoh massage sandals for daily relief (reflexology nodes stimulate circulation)

## Morton's Neuroma

**What it is:** A thickening of the tissue around one of the nerves leading to the toes — most often between the 3rd and 4th metatarsal heads. The thickened nerve tissue causes burning, numbness, and a sensation of "stepping on a marble" or a "bunched-up sock."

**Symptoms:** Burning or sharp pain in the ball of the foot, tingling or numbness in two adjacent toes, sensation of something inside the ball of the foot, pain that worsens with narrow shoes or high heels.

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- Wide toe box shoes that eliminate lateral compression of the nerve
- Metatarsal pad in the orthotic, placed just proximal to the neuroma to splay the metatarsals and decompress the nerve
- Custom orthotics are highly effective for Morton's neuroma when combined with appropriate footwear

## Post-Tibial Tendonitis (PTTD)

**What it is:** The posterior tibial tendon runs along the inside of the ankle and supports the arch. When it becomes inflamed or tears, the arch collapses progressively — left untreated, PTTD leads to adult-acquired flatfoot.

**Symptoms:** Pain and swelling along the inner ankle and foot, arch collapse, difficulty standing on tiptoe on the affected foot, flat-footed appearance that has progressively worsened.

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- Custom orthotics with aggressive medial arch support and deep heel cup are the primary conservative treatment
- Stability footwear (Brooks Beast GTS, Xelero) to prevent further arch collapse
- Early intervention is critical — PTTD is progressive if unsupported. Many cases avoid surgery entirely with consistent orthotic use.

## Heel Spurs

**What it is:** A calcium deposit that forms a bony protrusion on the underside of the heel bone (calcaneus). Usually associated with plantar fasciitis — the spur forms at the attachment point of the plantar fascia in response to chronic strain.

**Important note:** The spur itself is often NOT the source of pain — the inflamed plantar fascia tissue around it is. Many people with heel spurs have no pain; many people with plantar fasciitis have no spurs on X-ray.

**Foot Solutions approach:** Same as plantar fasciitis — arch support, heel cushioning, and custom orthotics. The FS106 Plantar Fasciitis Pro insole is specifically designed for heel spur and plantar fasciitis relief.

## Achilles Tendinitis

**What it is:** Inflammation of the Achilles tendon — the large tendon connecting the calf muscles (gastrocnemius and soleus) to the heel bone. Common in runners and people who suddenly increase activity levels.

**Symptoms:** Stiffness and aching along the back of the heel, especially in the morning. Mild swelling. Pain that worsens with activity.

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- Heel lift to reduce tension on the Achilles by slightly elevating the heel and decreasing the stretch angle
- Custom orthotics with heel cushioning and mild elevation
- Shoes with moderate heel-to-toe drop (8–12mm) — Brooks GTS series is well-suited
- Avoid minimalist/zero-drop shoes during active tendinitis

## Diabetic Foot Health

Diabetes creates two serious foot-related risks: **neuropathy** (nerve damage causing numbness and loss of sensation) and **peripheral arterial disease** (reduced blood flow causing slow healing). Together, these mean that minor blisters, pressure sores, or rubbing can become serious ulcers if undetected.

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- Extra-depth shoes and diabetic-friendly insoles to eliminate pressure points
- Custom orthotics with accommodative (cushioning-first) design rather than functional (corrective) design — the goal is protection, not correction
- Regular foot self-examination and professional foot assessments are strongly recommended
- Aetrex diabetic footwear and insoles available in-store

**Note:** Diabetic customers should always involve their podiatrist or endocrinologist in orthotic decisions. Foot Solutions works cooperatively with medical providers.

## Arthritis and Foot Pain

**Osteoarthritis:** Cartilage breakdown in foot joints — most common in the big toe joint (hallux rigidus) and midfoot. Creates stiffness, pain, and reduced range of motion.

**Rheumatoid Arthritis:** Systemic autoimmune disease causing inflammation in multiple joints simultaneously. In the foot, RA often affects the forefoot first — metatarsalgia, toe deformities, and midfoot collapse.

**Foot Solutions approach:**
- Stiff-soled or rocker-bottom shoes reduce the painful joint movement during the gait cycle (Finn Comfort and Mephisto offer rocker-bottom options)
- Custom orthotics with arch support and metatarsal pads offload inflamed joints
- Accommodative orthotics for severe RA provide cushioning and pressure redistribution
- Work closely with the rheumatologist's recommendations

## The Chain Reaction: How Foot Problems Cause Knee, Hip, and Back Pain

Foot mechanics influence the entire kinetic chain:
- **Overpronation** → tibia rotates inward → knee tracks medially → IT band tightens → hip rotates → lumbar spine compensates
- **Supination** → tibia rotates outward → lateral knee stress → hip drops on the opposite side → scoliosis-like compensation
- **Leg length discrepancy** → pelvic tilt → lumbar scoliosis → chronic lower back pain

Correcting foot biomechanics with custom orthotics often resolves knee, hip, and back pain that years of chiropractic care or physical therapy alone could not fix — because the root cause was never addressed.
