## Custom Orthotics vs. Arch Supports: The Key Differences

Custom orthotics are personalized medical devices created using 3D laser scanning and gait analysis to address each individual's unique foot structure and movement patterns.

According to Mark Zajac, orthopedic and biomechanics expert at Foot Solutions: "Our bodies are not mirrored, and no two feet are the same." The process involves three steps: (1) 3D laser scanning to capture precise foot measurements, (2) gait analysis to understand pressure distribution and walking mechanics, (3) precision design and manufacturing — often completed within an hour in-store.

Over-the-counter arch supports are mass-produced for a generic foot and cannot address structural differences or specific medical conditions. Retail scanning systems only produce flat, 2D images — like "taking a photo to design a custom suit." These limitations mean OTC solutions cannot deliver the personalized support of a true custom orthotic.

Who benefits most from custom orthotics: people with asymmetrical feet (structural differences between left and right), active individuals and athletes, and anyone with specific foot or lower-body alignment conditions. Custom orthotics from Foot Solutions typically last 2-3 years, with check-ups ensuring continued effectiveness as foot structures evolve.

## Common Foot Injuries in Athletes — Insights from Dr. James Connelly

Dr. James Connelly, physical therapist and founder of Results Rehab, discussed common athletic foot injuries on the Get Aligned podcast.

**Turf Toe**: Occurs when the big toe becomes hyperextended — common in football, soccer, and basketball from forceful ground contact. Repetitive stress on the joint causes inflammation and pain. Can trigger compensatory movement patterns leading to knee, hip, or back pain. Early intervention is critical.

**Plantar Fasciitis in Athletes**: Inflammation of the thick tissue on the foot's bottom, causing sharp heel pain. Tight calves are a major contributing factor — they place abnormal stress on the plantar fascia. Treatment combines stretching, mobility exercises, and biomechanical correction.

**Prevention approach — "Prehab"**: Dr. Connelly emphasizes identifying and correcting poor biomechanics before injury occurs. Key prevention strategies: proper footwear with adequate support, biomechanical assessments, strength and flexibility training for calves/feet/ankles, appropriate rest and recovery protocols.

## Common Foot Issues Treated by Physical Therapists — Dr. Tiffany Lamonde

Dr. Tiffany Lamonde of Apex Wellness Providers shares insights on how physical therapists approach foot pain.

**Plantar Fasciitis and Heel Pain**: "Plantar fasciitis is a commonly cited diagnosis for heel pain, but it's often not the true cause." Many cases originate from lumbar spine issues. Simple interventions like arch taping (navicular sling) can help predict whether orthotics will be effective. Treatment includes targeted exercises and supportive footwear.

**Neuromas**: Nerve-related conditions causing persistent foot pain — even post-surgical. The source frequently traces to the lower back or nerve compression. Thorough diagnosis helps patients avoid unnecessary procedures.

**Foot Weakness from Over-Support**: Patients can become overly dependent on supportive footwear, neglecting natural foot strength. Physical therapists guide patients toward healthy balance through barefoot activities and progressive strengthening alongside orthotic use.

Dr. Lamonde treats patients holistically — as "a whole person" — incorporating nutrition, rehabilitation, and training. The most effective foot care combines professional evaluation, appropriate support, and active strengthening.

## Pregnancy and Feet: What Every Expecting Mother Should Know

Dr. Sherry P. Taylor, OBGYN and founder of Alpha Omega OBGYN, explains the significant foot changes during pregnancy.

**Physical changes**: Weight gain of 25-30 pounds adds substantial stress to feet, ankles, and knees. The relaxin hormone loosens ligaments, causing feet to spread and flatten. Increased blood volume causes swelling. Feet may permanently increase by half a size — many women find their feet do not return to pre-pregnancy size after childbirth.

**Management strategies**:
1. Supportive footwear with good cushioning and orthotic inserts to reduce pressure
2. Low-impact exercise (walking, prenatal yoga) to support circulation and joint health — keep heart rate around 140 bpm
3. Adequate hydration to reduce swelling
4. Avoid high heels — they shift weight to the ball of the foot and destabilize an already changing center of gravity

Foot Solutions recommends arch-supportive footwear from brands like Vionic, Aetrex, and Birkenstock during pregnancy, along with OTC insoles for additional arch support as the foot spreads.

## Caring for Your Feet to Prevent Blisters

Blisters are fluid-filled bubbles caused by friction between the foot and shoe. The body creates these protective pockets to shield irritated skin while it heals.

**Prevention**:
- Get feet professionally measured before purchasing shoes — many people wear the wrong size or width
- Shoes should feel comfortable immediately; do not assume they will break in
- Runners should replace shoes after 400-500 miles or 6 months — worn cushioning creates friction
- Change socks frequently to keep feet dry; wet feet dramatically increase blister formation
- Alternate between two pairs of shoes to allow complete drying between wears
- Watch for "hot spots" — red areas of friction — and cover them immediately with bandages

**Treatment**: Most blisters heal without intervention. For larger ones: sterilize a pin with rubbing alcohol, drain carefully, clean the area, and bandage. Diabetic patients or those with circulation issues should consult a medical professional before self-treatment.

Proper shoe fit — especially correct width and toe box depth — is the most effective blister prevention. A professional fitting at Foot Solutions includes width measurement, which many retail stores skip.

## What Are Bunions? Causes, Symptoms, and Treatment

A bunion is a prominent bony bump on the inside of the foot at the big toe joint, where the bone protrudes inward. A Tailor's bunion forms on the outside of the foot at the little toe joint.

**Causes**:
- Excessive inward foot rolling (overpronation) during walking
- Arthritic, genetic, and neuromuscular conditions
- Abnormal foot mechanics
- Improperly fitted shoes — especially narrow, pointed toe boxes
- High heels that shift weight forward and compress toes

**Symptoms**: Joint inflammation and soreness, big toe pain when walking, callus formation under the bump, difficulty fitting into shoes, general foot discomfort.

**Conservative treatments**:
- Wear properly fitted shoes with wide, deep toe boxes — this is essential
- Rocker-soled shoes to reduce motion at the big toe joint
- Custom orthotics to redistribute pressure away from the joint and slow progression
- Bunion shields and bandages to reduce friction
- Night splints to relieve joint stress
- Soaking feet in warm water for mild early-stage relief

**Key insight**: Orthotics cannot reverse a bunion (only surgery can correct the bony deformity), but they serve two critical purposes: (1) reducing pain by offloading the affected joint, and (2) slowing progression by correcting the biomechanical imbalance that is driving it. Wide toe box shoes and orthotics together are the most effective conservative approach.
