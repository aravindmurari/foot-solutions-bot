# Foot Solutions — Frequently Asked Questions

## General Questions

**Q: What exactly does Foot Solutions do?**
A: Foot Solutions is a foot wellness company specializing in custom orthotics, supportive footwear, and professional foot & gait analysis. Think of us as a blend between a medical foot specialist and a high-end comfort shoe retailer — but personalized and accessible, without the wait times of a doctor's office.

**Q: Do I need an appointment, or can I walk in?**
A: Walk-ins are always welcome at all Foot Solutions locations. For custom orthotic consultations — which take 30–45 minutes and involve a full foot scan and gait analysis — calling ahead to reserve a dedicated time slot is recommended so you get the full, unrushed experience.

**Q: Is there a fee for the foot and gait analysis?**

**Q: Do I have to buy something to get the analysis?**
A: No. The analysis is provided to help you understand your feet and what would help. We believe educated customers make the best decisions for their own health.

## Custom Orthotics

**Q: How much do custom orthotics cost?**
A: Custom orthotics at Foot Solutions are priced between $400 and $500, depending on the complexity of design and materials required.

**Q: Are custom orthotics covered by insurance or HSA/FSA?**
A: Many HSA (Health Savings Account) and FSA (Flexible Spending Account) plans cover custom orthotics — they are an eligible medical expense. Traditional health insurance coverage varies by plan. We recommend calling your plan administrator ahead of your visit. We can provide an itemized receipt for reimbursement submission.

**Q: How are Foot Solutions custom orthotics made?**
A: After your foot scan and gait analysis, our board-certified pedorthist designs your orthotic using digital modeling software. The design specifies arch height, heel cup depth, metatarsal pad placement, top cover material, and cushioning density. The orthotic is then 3D printed in-house — not sent to an outside lab. This means faster turnaround and easier in-house adjustments.

**Q: How long do custom orthotics last?**
A: With proper care, custom orthotics typically last 3+ years. Lifespan depends on your activity level (runners will wear them out faster than office workers), body weight, and care habits. Signs it's time to replace: visible compression of the support material, loss of arch contour, or return of original pain symptoms.

**Q: How are Foot Solutions custom orthotics different from ones from a podiatrist?**
A: The underlying technology is very similar — 3D scanning, individual design, custom manufacturing. Key differences: Foot Solutions orthotics are manufactured in-house with 3D printing (faster, more eco-friendly), and our board-certified pedorthists specialize exclusively in orthotics and footwear — it's their entire focus, not one tool in a broader medical practice. Prices are often comparable or lower, and we offer in-house adjustments.

**Q: What's the difference between custom orthotics and OTC insoles?**
A: OTC (over-the-counter) insoles are mass-produced for a generalized foot type. They come in small/medium/large and are not designed for your specific arch height, pressure distribution, or foot condition. Custom orthotics are designed from scratch for your foot specifically — like the difference between reading glasses from a drugstore and prescription eyeglasses from an optometrist.

**Q: My doctor gave me a prescription for orthotics. Can you fill it?**
A: Yes. Bring your prescription to any Foot Solutions location. Our pedorthist will review it and design the orthotic to meet the specifications your doctor requested. We work cooperatively with the medical community.

**Q: How long until I feel better after getting custom orthotics?**
A: Most patients notice improvement within 1–4 weeks. There is typically a 1–2 week break-in period where your foot adapts to the new support. Plantar fasciitis patients often report significant morning pain reduction within 2–4 weeks. Wear them 2–3 hours per day the first week, increasing gradually.

## Foot Conditions

**Q: I have plantar fasciitis. Can you help?**
A: Yes — plantar fasciitis is one of the most common conditions we address. We recommend starting with the Plantar Fasciitis Pro FS106 insole ($119.99) for immediate support, and pairing it with a stability shoe (Brooks GTS series). For persistent cases that haven't responded to OTC insoles, a custom orthotic is typically the most effective intervention. We also recommend stretching the plantar fascia and Achilles tendon daily.

**Q: I have flat feet / fallen arches. What do you recommend?**
A: Flat feet (overpronation) respond very well to arch support. Our Active FS104 insole is a good starting point. For more significant overpronation or pain that has progressed to the knee or hip, custom orthotics with medial arch control are the most effective solution. In-store gait analysis helps us determine the correct level of correction — too much can cause its own problems.

**Q: I have bunions. Can orthotics help if I don't want surgery?**
A: Orthotics cannot reverse a bunion (only surgery can correct the bony deformity), but they do two important things: (1) redistribute pressure away from the bunion joint, reducing pain significantly, and (2) slow the progression of the deformity by correcting the biomechanical imbalance that is driving it. Wide toe box shoes are equally important — we carry Finn Comfort, Mephisto, and other brands with the anatomic toe room bunion-prone feet need.

**Q: My back pain is not improving with chiropractic care. Could my feet be the cause?**
A: Very possibly. Foot mechanics influence the entire kinetic chain — ankle, knee, hip, and spine. Overpronation causes the tibia to rotate inward, which translates into knee malalignment, hip drop, and lumbar compensation. Many patients who have tried chiropractic, physical therapy, or massage for years see their back pain resolve after addressing the underlying foot biomechanics. A gait analysis can reveal if this is a factor.

**Q: I'm a runner and I keep getting shin splints. Can orthotics help?**
A: Yes. Shin splints (medial tibial stress syndrome) in runners are frequently linked to overpronation or rapid changes in training volume. Custom orthotics with motion control reduce the tibial rotation stress that drives shin splints. Pair with our Active FS104 insole or custom orthotics, and a stability running shoe like the Brooks Beast or Ariel GTS.

**Q: I have diabetes and my feet are numb. What do I need?**
A: Diabetic neuropathy creates serious foot health risks because minor pressure points can become ulcers without pain warning. We carry Aetrex diabetic footwear with extra depth, soft materials, and pressure-distributing insoles. Custom accommodative orthotics (cushioning-focused rather than corrective) are also available. We strongly recommend annual foot exams with your podiatrist. Please bring any doctor recommendations to your Foot Solutions visit.

**Q: My feet hurt after standing all day at work. What do you recommend?**
A: This is one of our most common customer scenarios. We recommend: (1) ComfortPro FS102 insoles ($119.99) as an immediate first step; (2) professional footwear with cushioned, rocker-bottom design — Dansko clogs are extremely popular with nurses and restaurant workers for exactly this reason; (3) if pain persists, a full gait analysis and custom orthotic consultation. Most occupational foot pain responds very well to proper insole support and appropriate footwear.

## Products & Shoes

**Q: What shoe brands do you carry?**
A: Brooks, Birkenstock, Aetrex, Finn Comfort, Vionic, Dansko, Naot, Mephisto, Drew Shoes, Kenkoh, NOENE, Xelero, New Balance, PB5Star, and Joola (pickleball). All brands are selected for foot health, comfort, and support quality.

**Q: I need wide shoes. Do you carry wide widths?**
A: Yes. We specialize in fitting wide feet. Brands like Drew Shoes (up to 10E width), New Balance, Xelero, and Aetrex offer extensive width ranges. We can assess your proper width in-store — many people unknowingly wear the wrong width, which contributes to bunions, hammer toes, and general discomfort.

**Q: Can I use your orthotics in my existing shoes?**
A: Custom orthotics are designed to work with specific shoe types. Bring your most frequently worn shoes to the consultation — the pedorthist will design the orthotic for that shoe type and assess whether the shoe provides adequate support as a partner to the orthotic. Some shoes are too flat, too flexible, or too narrow to work well with orthotics.

**Q: Can I order online?**
A: Our OTC insoles and footwear are available at shop.footsolutions.com. Custom orthotics require an in-person evaluation and are not available online. For local customers, we recommend visiting a Georgia store for the full Foot Solutions experience.
